My Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on creating a supportive and inclusive learning environment where students feel empowered to engage with challenging concepts. As I am primarily hoping to become a Grade 7–12 Math and Science teacher, I am passionate about disrupting negative perspectives about Math and helping students discover the beauty and logic behind mathematical concepts. I strive to create an engaging and supportive learning environment where students feel confident exploring problem-solving strategies and making connections between math and the real world. Through hands-on activities and real-life applications, I aim to inspire curiosity and help students develop a deeper appreciation for both Math and Physics.

Experience

My journey in teaching began in high school when I worked as a peer tutor, helping classmates navigate Math and Science. This early experience sparked a passion for education, which I carried into university, refining my teaching style along the way. As an undergraduate at Queen’s University, I worked as a teaching assistant at the Math Help Centre, providing drop-in support for students across all undergraduate Math and Statistics courses. After earning my Mathematics degree, I pursued a Master’s at Simon Fraser University, where I gained extensive teaching experience as a Teaching Assistant. During my Master’s, I had the opportunity to independently lead tutorial sections, allowing me to develop essential teaching skills such as lesson planning and classroom management. These graduate experiences solidified my passion for teaching, making it a natural choice to continue into a Bachelor of Education. I am now completing my BEd., actively participating in practicum sessions where I learn from experienced educators while honing my skills in preparation for a career in teaching.
